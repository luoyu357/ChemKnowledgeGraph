      Abstract

      Herein, we report a transition-metal-free annulation reaction of benzynes and 1,3-oxopentanedioate for the synthesis of highly functionalized naphthalene derivatives for the first time. Additionally, the representative naphthalene derivatives have been successfully transformed into the new series of rhodamine dye analogues.

      The synthesis of functionalized polycyclic aromatic compounds has always been of immense interest to organic chemists, owing to their wide utility in organic, medicinal, and material applications. (1) Among these, functionalized naphthalenes of type A are important fluorophores (2) and are valuable intermediates in the synthesis of complex target molecules such as Nile red dyes, rhodamine dyes, and azo-dyes, and also, they are involved in the synthesis of several amide linkers that are required in the solid-phase synthesis (Figure 1). (3) In addition, they are an important starting material for the preparation of medicinally important hypoglycemic agents, (4) serotonin 5-HT1A inhibitors, etc. (5) Despite the multifunctional utility of this core, however, approaches for its synthesis are very limited, and often they are laborious, multistep processes and require the use of metals. (6) Therefore, the development of novel and efficient one-step strategy to access these kinds of valuable intermediates from readily available starting material is ideal and highly desirable. (7)
      Over the past few decades, transition-metal-free organic transformations have received a great deal of attention over the conventional synthetic methods, allowing the expeditious and sustainable formation of diverse chemical scaffolds. (8) Nevertheless, the design and development of novel benzannulated analogues via aryne-based chemistry, (9) especially in the absence of transition metals has tremendous potential. Recently, various types of aryne annulation strategy have been developed under metal-catalyzed or metal-free conditions because they facilitate the Ï-extension in a single step, and the final products are very important in terms of synthetic and applications perspectives. (10) In particular, the [2+2+2] cycloaddition reaction is one of the appropriate and useful strategies for the construction of a naphthalene core. (11) Such as, Xie and Yamamoto et al. described the synthesis of substituted naphthalene derivatives employing nickel- and palladium-catalyzed coupling reactions between aryneâalkyneâalkene, respectively. (12) In addition, the aryneâalkyneâalkyne cocyclization reaction catalyzed by palladium also gives access to a variety of polysubstituted naphthalene rings. (13) Further, Huang and co-workers reported the synthesis of polysubstituted naphthols and naphthalenes using a multicomponent reaction between arynes, Î²-keto sulfones, and Michael-type acceptors. (14) In 2016, Wu and co-workers reported the synthesis of polyfunctional naphthalene derivatives using an aryneâalkyneâketone coupling annulation reaction. (15) Treatment of arynes with diketo compounds is found to be the alternate strategy to construct the naphthalene system. With this strategy, the research group of Stoltz and co-workers reported the synthesis of naphthoquinones via a one-pot aryne acyl-alkylation/condensation employing arynes with diketo compounds (Scheme 1, eq 1). (16) In 2012, Okuma et al. reported an interesting example for the synthesis of napthols using an intramolecular aldol reaction with arynes and diketo compounds (Scheme 1, eq 2). (17) Intrigued by Stoltz and Okumaâs work on arynes with diketo compounds, we have sought to examine whether engaging a benzannulation reaction of aryne with a triketo compound would deliver synthetically valuable functionalized 1,3-dinaphthol derivatives. Herein, we report a transition-metal-free synthesis of a functionalized naphthalene core, i.e., 1,3-dihydroxy-2-naphthoate, utilizing the aryne precursor and triketo substrate (Scheme 1, eq 3). To the best of our knowledge, no report of direct benzannulation of triketo compounds with arynes exists.
      The present study commenced with the treatment of aryne generated from 2-(trimethylsiliyl)aryl triflate 1a (18) and diethyl 3-oxopentanedioate 2a using CsF as a fluoride source in CH3CN solvent at room temperature. Encouragingly, these conditions did afford the desired benzannulated product 3a, but in a low yield. Pleasingly, a significant increase in the yield of 3a was observed while increasing the temperature: 50 Â°C, 28%; 80 Â°C, 71% (Table 1, entries 2 and 3). With these promising results in hand, we next examined the compatibility of other solvents like THF, CHCl3, and toluene (Table 1, entries 4â6) and found that CH3CN was the best solvent for the present annulation reaction. Similarly, the impacts of the alternative fluorine sources such as KF and TBAF on the benzannulation reaction were also examined, and they are found to be inefficient in improving the reaction efficiency (Table 1, entries 7 and 8). Further, the addition of an additive such as 18-crown-6 was not found to be beneficial in this transformation (Table 1, entry 9). Furthermore, increasing the stoichiometric quantity of CsF also did not enhance the yield of the product further (Table 1, entry 10). No product formation was observed in the absence of CsF (Table 1, entry 11). Overall, the optimized conditions consisted of performing the reaction in a CH3CN solvent in the presence of CsF (3.0 equiv) at 80 Â°C for 12 h.

      With the established optimal conditions, the scope of the reaction was examined with various arynes. As shown in Table 2, annulation of tricarbonyl derivatives with arynes proceeded well to give the desired products 3 irrespective of the substitution patterns on the phenyl ring of arynes. Both electron-donating and halo substituents, such as methyl (3câ3f), fluoro (3iâ3j), and methoxy (3kâ3l) were compatible, provided the corresponding 1,3-dihydroxy-2-naphthoates in moderate to good yields. The reaction was well tolerated with a high electron-rich aryne-containing methylene-dioxy substituent at the phenyl ring, giving 3m and 3n in 81 and 76% yields, respectively. The presence of an additional cyclic ring system on an aryne precursor does not hamper the reaction and afforded the corresponding 1,3-dihydroxy-2-naphthoate (3gâ3h, in 65 and 64% yields). Further, replacing the benzene ring with a naphthalene backbone in the aryne precursor smoothly reacts with diethyl 3-oxopentanedioate, and the desired products 3o and 3p were obtained in 71% and 67% yields, respectively. The methyl and methoxy substituent-bearing unsymmetrical arynes also work well to form the desired products 3qâ3s in good yields with regioisomer ratios of 1.3:1, 1.3:1, and 3.5:1, respectively. It was also observed that an electron-withdrawing group such as NO2- and CF3-bearing arynes was incompatible for this benzannulation reaction and failed to yield the desired products. Importantly, as expected, these 1,3-dihydroxy-2-naphthoate analogues exhibit a bright fluorescent intensity (see Supporting Information).

      Based on previous reports, a plausible reaction pathway of the reaction is depicted in Scheme 2. At first, the nucleophilic attack of an in situ generated enolate of 2a to the aryne led the production of the aryl anion intermediate B. Subsequent intramolecular nucleophilic attack on the carbonyl moiety formed intermediate C. Intermediate C undergoes a base-induced Claisen reaction to form intermediate D, which results in intermediate E through condensation of the ester functionality. Further, intermediate E on ketoâenol tautomerization forms the desired product 3a. Further, the scalability of the benzannulation reaction was also tested by performing the reaction of the aryne precursor 1a on a 0.6 g scale. The reaction worked very well, furnishing 3a in 62% yield, which indicates that this method is suitable for the gram-scale synthesis of functionalized naphthalene for a further utility purpose.
      To demonstrate the synthetic utility of the new method, we applied the 1,3-dihydroxy-2-naphthoate to the synthesis of various hitherto unknown asymmetric rhodamine dye analogues 5bâ5f (Scheme 3). Accordingly, upon exposure of the NEt2-bearing keto acid 4 to the 1,3-dihydroxy-2-naphthoates (3) in the presence of trifluoroacetic acid followed by esterification reaction, asymmetric rhodamine dye analogues 5aâ5f were obtained in good yields. (19) The synthesized rhodamine dye analogues exhibit a bright fluorescent emission, and their photophysical properties have been studied in CH2Cl2 at room temperature (Figure 2). All of the rhodamine dye analogues have absorption maxima around 530â540 nm, and the emission range covers the visible region, offering an orange palette of colors in CH2Cl2 (Î»em 560â586 nm, Table S2). These compounds have good photoluminescence capabilities with Î¦f values ranging between 0.34 and 0.63. It should be noted that such class of fluorescent rhodamine dyes exhibits multiple applications in the field of biology as fluorescent probes. (20)
      In summary, we have developed an extremely simple protocol for the synthesis of a highly functionalized naphthalene core, i.e, 1,3-dihydroxy-2-naphthoate, using a novel one-pot operation from easily accessible aryne precursors. The photophysical studies of the representative analogues indicate that these compounds exhibit good photoluminescence properties. In addition, this novel and valid annulation has been successfully applied to the synthesis of several new asymmetric rhodamine dyes. Importantly, these 1,3-dihydroxy-2-naphthoate scaffolds with multiple functional group handles are suitable for further elaboration to generate molecular complexity.

      Experimental Procedures

      General Procedure for the Preparation of Various Aryne Precursors. (21)

      ortho-Silyl aryltriflates were prepared according to the previously reported literature procedure: To an oven-dried two-necked flask, 2-bromophenol (20 mmol, 1.0 equiv) in THF (50 mL) and HMDS (22 mmol 1.1 equiv) were added under a N2 atmosphere. After the reaction was refluxed for 3 h, the system was concentrated under reduced pressure, and the crude product obtained was carried forward to the next step without further purification. The crude product (10.0 mmol, 1.0 equiv) and THF (30.0 mL) were added to an oven-dried two-necked flask under a N2 atmosphere and cooled to â78 Â°C. Then, 4 mL of n-BuLi (1.6 M in hexane) was added dropwise, and the mixture was stirred for 2 h at â78 Â°C. Then trifluoromethanesulfonic anhydride (11 mmol, 1.1 equiv) was added dropwise, and the mixture was stirred for another 1 h at â78 Â°C. Then the mixture was quenched by saturated NaHCO3 and stirred for another 4 h at room temperature. The mixture was extracted with EtOAc three times, dried over Na2SO4, and concentrated under reduced pressure. The residue was purified by column chromatography on silica gel (petroleum ether) to give aryne precursors.

      General Procedure for the Preparation 1,3-Dihydroxy-2-naphthoates

      To a screw-cap vial containing a stir bar were added aryne precursor (0.15 mmol, 1.0 equiv), 3-oxopentanedioate ester (0.18 mmol, 1.2 equiv), CsF (3.0 equiv), and dry CH3CN (2 mL). The crude residue was purified by column chromatography on silica gel (230â400 mesh) to afford the corresponding substituted 1,3-dihydroxy-2-naphthoates 3 in moderate to good yields.
